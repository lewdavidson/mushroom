Morel

  • Origin: France, Germany, USA
  • Edible: Yes! Beware of imposters!
  • ID Tips: Cup is longer than the stem, hollow on the inside, available only in spring. Toxic look-alikes will have stuffing inside, the cap is loose from the stem and equal in size.
  • Pairs Well With: Butter, Garlic, White Meat. They are best simply sauted in butter with good salt but can be added to delicate protiens that won't mask their flavor.

Chanterelle

  • Origin:North America
  • Edible: Yes! But beware of imposters!
  • ID Tips: Bright yellow-gold in color, always found near trees, gills are solidly part of the cap and stem (not removeable). Imposters include the Jack-O-Lantern and "False Chanterelle" mushrooms. They are both more orange in color with removeable gills.
  • Pairs Well With: Butter and garlic. Make sure they are dried well so you can ent up with a crispy-edged tender mushroom!
  • Learn More

Hen of the Woods

  • Origin: China, Japan, and North America
  • Edible: Yes!
  • ID Tips: These mushrooms grow at the base of trees in clusters. They are often said to resemble a roosting hen against a tree, hence the name! They are identifiable by their spoon shaped cups and brown color.
  • Pairs Well With: Risotto, Eggs, Pasta. Peel them apart into strips!

Turkey Tail

  • Origin: China
  • Edible: No
  • ID Tips: This mushroom resembles a fanned out turkey's tail whith brown or reddish rings. On the underside you will find small holes that house the spores for this fan fungi!
  • Pairs Well With: Not goot to eat, is known to e an immune-booster, but doesn't have a very plesant flavor.

Lobster Mushroom

  • Origin: North America
  • Edible: Yes
  • ID Tips: This mushroom is known to resemble the shell of a cooked lobster (bright red) with a white interior. There are riged gills on the underside with a small cup on the top!
  • Pairs Well With: Seafood, butter, garlic. Lobster mushrooms are said to have a seafood-type flavor when cooked.

Lion's Mane

  • Origin: North America, Europe, Asia
  • Edible: Yes!
  • ID Tips: A very distinct mushroom! Is covered in what looks like a mine of bright white hair that grows longer as the mushroom lives. This mushroom is usually found on a rotting log.
  • Pairs Well With: Smoke. This hearty mushroom can stand up to some time in a smoker or even a slice fried in butter and put on a sandwich!

    • Edible: NO!
